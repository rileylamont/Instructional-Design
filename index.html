Course Description:

This course is designed to empower learners to build a strong and sustainable work ethic by identifying and overcoming the most common barriers to personal and professional discipline. Through practical strategies, real-world examples, and reflective exercises, participants will gain a deeper understanding of the psychological, emotional, and environmental obstacles that hinder consistent effort and motivation. By the end of the course, learners will be equipped with actionable tools to remove these roadblocks and cultivate habits that support long-term growth, productivity, and personal integrity.

Course Metaphor

Imagine you’re on a road trip. You have a cracked windshield. It’s irritating, but you can still see. You keep driving… until your car suddenly breaks down. At that point, it doesn’t matter how clean the windshield is. If the engine isn’t working, you’re not going anywhere. Most people spend their time trying to fix the "windshield"—cleaning up distractions, organizing their schedule, or adding productivity tools. But if your engine isn’t running—your mindset, your emotions, your internal drive—then none of that matters. This course is designed to help you: Remove internal blocks like negative thoughts, emotional resistance, and pride Regain control of your focus Build the internal strength to keep going, even when it’s hard Once the engine is running, the journey becomes possible; you’ll be able to handle those windshield cracks along the way.

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course, you will be able to: Identify and remove negative thought patterns that block motivation Recognize and regulate difficult emotions like anxiety and burnout Overcome both internal and external distractions with practical focus tools Pinpoint how pride, ego, or insecurity may be quietly sabotaging your effort—and learn how to disarm them This isn’t about getting work done today. It’s about removing what’s been stopping you from becoming who you’re meant to be.

Biblical Foundation

Ecclesiastes 9:10: “Whatsoever thy hand findeth to do, do it with thy might; for there is no work, nor device, nor knowledge, nor wisdom, in the grave, whither thou goest.” The Lord has instructed us to give our best effort in everything we pursue. Learning strategies to increase our ability to work will help us better follow God’s direction.
