Mindset

Your mindset affects the way you think, feel, and act.

The think→feel→act cycle can be represented by a bicycle wheel. Removing a negative mindset is like sticking a metal rod into the wheel. It's going to crash, and it may not be pleasant at first. Stopping this cycle is the biggest barrier to cultivating a strong work ethic, so if we can get this out of the way first, everything else will come much smoother.
The purpose of this module is to remove barriers to a strong work ethic that are related to the way you think.

Before we can develop a positive mindset, we need to remove a negative one.
Before we can work on removing a negative mindset, we need to define some terms and get some background knowledge.

Thought

A thought is an idea or opinion. To have a thought is to connect ideas together.

Belief

To believe something is to accept it as true.

Important clarification - Something isn’t true just because you believe it.

Mindset

Mindset is your default way of thinking or general attitude toward something.

How Are They Connected?

A thought can be related to the weather on any given day. Today it is rainy, tomorrow might be sunny—I have a negative thought right now, but the next one might be positive.

A belief is similar to an area’s climate. It rains almost everyday, so the climate is humid. Climate is simply longterm weather. Belief, similarly, is long term thoughts. If you generally have a negative attitude toward uncomfortable situations, you have a pretty nasty climate/belief.

Mindset is the combination of these factors. A humid climate rains most days, and places that rain most days are considered a humid climate. Constant negative thoughts create negative beliefs, and negative beliefs reinforce negative thought. You cannot have one without the other.

Remember:
Negative thoughts → negative beliefs → negative mindset
