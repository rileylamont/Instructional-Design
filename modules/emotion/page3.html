Emotion

Strategies

I’ve already given you the first—and probably most important—strategy in the Mindset module. We can use the same tactics we applied to removing negative thoughts to also remove negative beliefs.

To put it simply, follow this process:

  1. Recognize the emotion – “What am I feeling right now?”
  2. Label the emotion – “That is a negative emotion.”
  3. Acknowledge the emotion without judgment – “It’s okay to feel this way.”
  4. Ask – “How is this emotion helping me?”

These steps are simple, yet powerful. Remember: don’t judge yourself or beat yourself up for feeling anything—it's normal. Acknowledge it. Accept it. Otherwise, you’re letting in additional negativity through the back door. Sincerely question the emotion: will it benefit you in any way? That’s the core practice.

Now let’s apply it to specific emotional states you might encounter while trying to work:

Anxiety

Anxiety shows up when we imagine everything going wrong at once. It’s being overwhelmed by the future.

The best way to work with anxiety is to break it into smaller pieces. Write down what needs to get done. If you’re still struggling to start, break the tasks down even further. Tell yourself: “I only have to do this little bit right now.”

Depression

Depression steals your energy and makes tasks seem pointless. Remember, it is caused by your ego—it is a lie. It is not who you are.

Many feelings we experience from depression begin with a depressing thought that we've grown used to thinking. When you notice such a thought, shine a light on it. Accept it, and ask: “How is this thought helping me?” If you can disrupt the thoughts, you can weaken depression’s hold.

Overwhelm

Overwhelm or burnout often comes from trying to process too much at once.

Recognize that you’re overwhelmed. Accept it—don’t fight it. Ask how that feeling benefits you. Then, chop up your work into small pieces. Break tasks down until you know you can finish the first one. Then do the next. Don’t worry about the big picture—just focus on what you can do right now.
