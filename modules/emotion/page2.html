Emotion

Your emotions affects the way you think, feel, and act.

We are at the feel section of the think→feel→act cycle In our last module (think), we discussed removing negative thoughts and beliefs. In this module, we are going to remove negative emotions (feel). The last module did not focus on developing a positive mindset, just removing a negative one.

Similarly, we are not trying to develop any positive emotion yet, as forcing that will just make you miserable. Instead, we are going to take the easy, effective route, and solely focus on removing the bad.

Key terms:

Ego:

In a psychological setting, the ego is the part of your brain that is trying to protect you, it sees change as something dangerous.


Emotional Regulation:

The ability to effectively control and respond to your feelings.


How They Are Connected:

Your mind is capable of changing, therefore, you are able to regulate your emotions. In other words, you can control how you feel.

Immediately when I say that, there may be some sirens going off in your mind. You may be thinking of reasons why that statement is untrue. That voice you are hearing is not you, it is your ego. Your ego likely told you that you cannot control your feelings, it does that because it is afraid of change. Fortunately, you do not have to listen to it!

The next page will guide you through how to regulate your emotions while working. Most strategies you’ll find online involve doing something outside of you—like taking a walk, getting fresh air, or calling a friend. But when you're in the middle of a task, a class, or a deadline, you don’t always have the luxury of stepping away. So this module focuses on strategies for what to do when you can’t do anything else. Inner strategies. Mental tools. And they work—even when you’re stuck at a desk.
