Strategies

Now that you’ve seen how pride can be manifested, let’s talk about how to disarm it and grow beyond it.

1. Overcoming Avoidance

Avoidance is part of the behavioral avoidance cycle:

Task makes you uncomfortable → You avoid it → You feel temporary relief → Avoidance is reinforced

Break the loop:

  • Use exposure: Do the task anyway, even if it’s uncomfortable.
  • Name the emotion: Are you avoiding fear, failure, or embarrassment?
  • Reflect: “What’s the worst that could happen? Can I handle it?”

2. Reversing Helplessness

Helplessness is often learned—and that means it can be unlearned.

  • Acknowledge the false belief that "I can't change this."
  • Focus on small wins: Confidence grows through successful action.
  • Repeat positive phrases: “I can do hard things.”

3. Challenging False Identity

Outdated self-labels are not facts—they're mental shortcuts (Schema Activation) built from past feedback.

  • Notice when you say things like “I’m not good at___.”
  • Ask: “Is this really true, or just a belief I’ve accepted?”
  • Replace it with: “I am capable of changing and learning.”

4. Increasing Self-Awareness

The more you see, the more you can grow. Use this process:

  • Recognize a limiting thought or emotion
  • Label it
  • Acknowledge it without judgment
  • Ask: “Is this helping me, or holding me back?”

As you do this, you shrink your blind spots—and your ego has less room to hide.

Final Thoughts

Letting go of ego and pride isn’t about destroying yourself—it’s about becoming free. Free to grow. Free to change. But only when you stop defending a false version of yourself.

You’re not just removing barriers—you’re rewriting what you can achieve.

"The curious paradox is that when I accept myself just as I am, then I can change." — Carl Rogers
